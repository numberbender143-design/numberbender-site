- **Where they spend time:** YouTube, TikTok, Google Search, Reddit (r/learnmath, r/homeworkhelp), Discord study servers

- **Search behavior:** High-intent, topic-specific ("how to solve systems of equations step by step", "chain rule practice problems pdf")

**Secondary Segment:** Teachers and tutors looking for ready-made worksheets and explainers to share with students.

## 4. Channel Strategy

### SEO / Blog Content (Owned) — Priority: HIGH
- **Why:** High school students start on Google. Ranking for math topics = consistent, compounding traffic.
- **Format:** Long-form explainer posts (1,000–2,000 words) targeting specific math questions students actually search
- **Effort:** Medium (ongoing; build a library of 50–100 posts over 6 months)
- **Examples:** "How to factor quadratics — 3 methods explained", "Derivative rules cheat sheet", "Unit circle memorization tricks"

### Worksheet Downloads (Owned) — Priority: HIGH
- **Why:** Downloadable PDFs are highly shareable and rank well for "[topic] worksheet pdf" searches
- **Format:** Branded PDF worksheets with answer keys, tied to each content post
- **Effort:** Low-Medium (already have a production workflow; scale output)

### YouTube (Owned) — Priority: HIGH
- **Why:** Students already watch math on YouTube; Numberbender videos are the entry point, site is the resource hub
- **Format:** Cross-promote site content in video descriptions; "worksheet in the link below" CTA
- **Effort:** Low (leverage existing videos; add site links systematically)

### TikTok / YouTube Shorts (Owned) — Priority: MEDIUM
- **Why:** Discovery channel for a younger audience who don't start on Google
- **Format:** 30–60 second "aha moment" clips — one concept, one trick, one visual
- **Effort:** Medium (repurpose existing video content into short clips)

### Email Newsletter (Owned) — Priority: MEDIUM
- **Why:** Converts one-time visitors into repeat visitors; keeps students coming back before each test
- **Format:** Bi-weekly "Test Prep Drop" — 2 worksheets, 1 explainer, 1 quick tip
- **Effort:** Low (template-based; pairs with existing content production)

### Reddit / Community (Earned) — Priority: LOW-MEDIUM
- **Why:** r/learnmath, r/homeworkhelp, and Discord math servers are where students ask questions — being genuinely helpful there builds awareness
- **Format:** Answer questions helpfully; share relevant worksheets when directly applicable (not spam)
- **Effort:** Low (15–30 min/week)
